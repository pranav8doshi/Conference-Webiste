Guidelines for Crafting a
Compelling Tenure Presentation

--This is sample text--A tenure presentation is an essential part of the evaluation process for tenure-track faculty members. It provides an opportunity to demonstrate your achievements in research, teaching, and service. The presentation should effectively communicate your contributions and future plans, showcasing your commitment and impact in your field.

Rules

  • Relevance: Ensure that all content is directly related to your research, teaching, and service.
  • Clarity: Present information clearly and concisely to facilitate understanding.
  • Professionalism: Maintain a formal tone and style throughout the presentation.

Guidelines for Preparing PPTs

  • Structure: Organize your presentation with a clear introduction, body, and conclusion. Include sections on research achievements, teaching contributions, and service activities.
  • Visuals: Use visuals like charts, graphs, and images to support your points. Ensure they are high quality and relevant.
  • Text: Keep text minimal and to the point. Use bullet points and short phrases rather than long paragraphs.
  • Consistency: Maintain a consistent design and font style throughout the presentation for a cohesive look.

Time & Slide Limit

  • Duration: Your presentation should typically last between 20-30 minutes, followed by a Q&A session.
  • Slides: Aim for approximately 15-20 slides to keep your presentation focused and within the time limit. Avoid overcrowding slides with information.

Video Limit

  • Length: If including videos, ensure they are brief (no longer than 2-3 minutes) and directly relevant to your presentation.
  • Quality: Videos should be of high quality and tested to ensure they play smoothly during the presentation.

What is Group Discussion ?

Group Discussion (GD) is a collaborative exercise where participants discuss a specific topic or scenario to evaluate their communication, teamwork, and problem-solving skills. It is often used in academic and professional settings to gauge participants' ability to articulate their thoughts and engage in constructive dialogue.
